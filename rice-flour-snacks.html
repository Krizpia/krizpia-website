Rice Flour Snacks Guide

Rice flour is the base of many Kerala snacks because it creates a light, crisp bite when mixed, shaped and fried correctly.

Why rice flour works for crispy snacks

Rice flour has a mild taste, smooth texture and excellent crunch after frying. In Kerala homes, it is used for snacks that need a clean flavour and long-lasting crispness. Krizpia uses rice-flour based recipes to keep snacks familiar, light and suitable for tea-time sharing.

Kuzhalappam is one of the best examples of a rice flour snack. The dough is rolled thin, shaped into tubes and fried until crisp. Thatta uses a flatter shape, giving a different bite while keeping the same Kerala-style crunch.

Storage and serving tips

Store Krizpia snacks in a cool, dry place away from sunlight, moisture and strong odours. After opening, keep the pack sealed or transfer the snacks to an airtight container to maintain crispness.

Serve with tea, coffee, fresh fruit or other Kerala snacks. For family plates, combine Traditional Kuzhalappam, Spicy Kuzhalappam, Sweet Kuzhalappam and Thatta so guests can choose their preferred taste.
