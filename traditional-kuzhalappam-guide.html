What is Kuzhalappam? The Complete Guide to Kerala's Iconic Rice Flour Snack

Kuzhalappam is a traditional Kerala rice flour snack known for its crisp tube shape, light crunch and familiar tea-time flavour.

What is Kuzhalappam?

Kuzhalappam is a crispy, tube-shaped Kerala snack made mainly with rice flour, coconut and traditional spices. The dough is rolled thin, wrapped into small hollow tubes and fried until it becomes crunchy.

The name comes from the Malayalam words for a tube or pipe shape and a traditional snack. That shape is what makes Kuzhalappam easy to recognise on a Kerala tea-time plate.

What makes Kuzhalappam special?

Kuzhalappam stands out because it combines a simple rice flour base with a delicate crunch and mild spice notes from ingredients such as cumin, fennel and sesame. It is lighter than many heavy sweets and pairs naturally with hot tea or coffee.

It is a good choice for families who want a familiar Kerala snack for daily tea-time, guests, gifting or travel. Krizpia offers Traditional, Spicy and Sweet Kuzhalappam varieties so customers can choose a classic, bold or gentler flavour.

Frequently asked questions

What is Kuzhalappam?

Kuzhalappam is a traditional crispy Kerala snack made mainly from rice flour, coconut and spices. It is rolled into a tube shape and fried until crunchy, making it a popular tea-time snack.

What is Kuzhalappam made of?

Kuzhalappam is usually made with rice flour, coconut, cumin, fennel, sesame seeds, salt and oil. Ingredients may vary slightly by recipe and flavour.

How do you eat Kuzhalappam?

Kuzhalappam is commonly enjoyed as a crunchy snack with tea or coffee. It can also be served on family snack plates with other Kerala snacks such as Thatta.
