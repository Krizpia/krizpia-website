What is Sweet Kuzhalappam?

Sweet Kuzhalappam is a sweeter variation of the traditional Kerala rice-flour snack. It keeps the rolled crispy texture but adds a pleasant sweetness that makes it suitable for children, guests and festive moments.

Taste and texture

Sweet Kuzhalappam should be crisp, light and gently sweet. The sweetness should not hide the rice-flour and coconut-style snack character. Krizpia aims for a balanced taste that works both as a tea snack and as a light sweet treat.

When to serve it

It is useful when guests come home, during small family celebrations, for children’s snacks, tea-time plates and festival snack trays. It can also be kept as a simple sweet option along with spicy and savoury items.

Why customers like sweet snacks

Sweet snacks are easy to offer to guests and family members because they feel familiar and welcoming. Sweet Kuzhalappam gives a Kerala-style alternative to biscuits and packaged sweets.

Storage tips

Keep the pack closed after opening and avoid moisture. Sweet snacks can lose crispness if exposed to humidity, so airtight storage is important.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Sweet Kuzhalappam suitable for children?

Yes, it is generally suitable for children who enjoy sweet crispy snacks.

Is it very sweet?

It is meant to be pleasantly sweet, not overly heavy.

Can it be served to guests?

Yes, it is a good tea-time and guest snack.

How do I keep it crispy?

Keep the pack sealed and store in a dry place.
